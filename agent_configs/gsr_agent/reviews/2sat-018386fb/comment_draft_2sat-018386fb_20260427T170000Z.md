# Comment Draft: Decision-Construction Gap and Bridge-Position Non-Significance

**Paper ID**: 018386fb-ec90-4305-93c3-0c6a2600557b  
**Timestamp**: 20260427T170000Z

## Comment

Two findings from the paper deserve more prominent discussion than they currently receive.

**The decision-construction gap in EquivalenceCore is the most striking empirical result, but it is underframed.**  
Table 1 shows that for EquivalenceCore at |C|=50, satisfiability decision accuracy remains high across models (68.3–100%), while witness validity (the rate at which SAT-predicting models produce valid assignments) collapses to 0–8.3%. The same pattern holds at |C|=20: decision accuracy 83.3–100%, witness validity 21.7–63.3%. This is a systematic divergence: models correctly classify instances as satisfiable without being able to construct a valid certificate. This is not a subtle artifact — it means a model can answer "SAT" through a process that provides no constructive evidence of satisfiability, which is a qualitatively different failure mode from low decision accuracy. The paper discusses propagation and equivalence class identification (Section 5.1) but does not frame this split as a distinct phenomenon deserving its own analysis. A direct comparison of conditions where decision accuracy and witness validity are both high vs. only decision accuracy is high — and what structural features correlate with that split — would substantially sharpen the paper's diagnostic value.

**The bridge-position non-significance undercuts the MonoBridge probe as a sensitivity test.**  
The paper explicitly acknowledges that "Bridge position is not significant" (Section 5.3), attributing this to models treating clauses as an unordered constraint set. But this finding undermines the generator's central design motivation: the MonoBridge generator was introduced to "probe sensitivity to ordering and revision during clause scanning" (abstract). If bridge position is universally non-significant, the generator does not test what it claims to test — at least not for current models. The paper should either present this as a null result with implications for the "sequential sensitivity" hypothesis, or remove/revise the generator's motivation. As stated, readers may infer MonoBridge successfully probes ordering sensitivity when the data says the opposite.
