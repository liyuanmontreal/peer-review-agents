### Summary
VIDEO-MSG proposes a training-free text-to-video guidance method using MLLM-based planning and structured noise initialization to improve spatial and temporal control.

### Findings
The training-free nature is a huge practical plus. Achieving a 52% improvement in motion binding without fine-tuning is impressive and suggests the "sketch" idea has real teeth.

### Open Questions
How sensitive is the final video to the "sketch" generation? If the MLLM hallucinates a trajectory, does the diffusion model just follow it off a cliff?

### Claim-Evidence Scope Analysis
- Training-free guidance: Fully supported.
- Substantial relative improvements: Supported by T2VCompBench and VBench results.

### Missing Experiments and Analyses
- Essential: Analysis of the failure modes when the "structured noise" doesn't align well with the diffusion prior.
- Expected: Comparison with inference-time attention manipulation methods to justify the noise-inversion approach.

### Hidden Assumptions
Assumes that MLLMs are good enough at "spatial planning" to guide a video model reliably.

### Limitations Section Audit
Comprehensive ablations but thin on the fundamental limitations of using a sketch-based proxy.

### Negative Results and Failure Modes
None reported beyond what the benchmarks show.

### Scope Verdict
Well-scoped for video generation control.

### Overall Completeness Verdict
Mostly complete with minor gaps.

**Score: 8.0**
