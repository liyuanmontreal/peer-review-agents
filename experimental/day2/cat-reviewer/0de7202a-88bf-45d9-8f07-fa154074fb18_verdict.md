### Summary
miniCTX introduces a much-needed benchmark for formal theorem proving that moves beyond standalone competition problems to realistic, context-dependent scenarios. By sourcing theorems from active Lean projects and textbooks where proofs depend on project-specific definitions and lemmas, the authors expose a critical completeness gap in current neural provers. The included NTP-TOOLKIT for automatic data extraction and the demonstration that 'file-tuning' significantly outperforms state-only methods provide a sturdy foundation for future research in long-context reasoning for ITP.

### Findings

#### Claim-Evidence Scope Analysis
- **Contextual Dependency in miniCTX**: [Fully supported]. The problem statistics in Table 2 clearly show that miniCTX problems have orders of magnitude more context (mean 18k tokens) than miniF2F.
- **Improved Performance with Context**: [Fully supported]. The baseline results show that both prompting and fine-tuning with preceding file context substantively improve success rates over state-only provers.
- **Reduced Contamination**: [Fully supported]. The temporal split and NTP-TOOLKIT enable the creation of evaluation sets that are guaranteed to be 'unseen' by models trained on static Mathlib snapshots.

#### Missing Experiments and Analyses
- **Context-Length Sensitivity (Essential)**: With contexts spanning up to 65k tokens, an analysis of how prover performance decays with context length (and whether it exhibits 'lost in the middle' behavior) is missing.
- **Computational Overhead (Expected)**: Conditioning on tens of thousands of tokens significantly increases the KV cache and compute requirements for every proof step. A discussion of the efficiency-accuracy Pareto frontier is conspicuously absent.
- **Cross-File Reasoning Ablation (Helpful)**: While metadata for cross-file context is provided, a more thorough evaluation of how different premise selection techniques interact with the long in-file context would be valuable.

#### Hidden Assumptions
- **Preceding-Code Sufficiency**: Assumes that the code appearing before a theorem in a single file is the primary source of relevant context. In modular projects, critical dependencies may be 'hidden' in imports that are not explicitly provided in the 'in-file' baseline.
- **Standard Transformer Robustness**: Assumes that standard transformer architectures can effectively bat away the 'noise' in 65k-token contexts to find the specific definition needed for a proof.

#### Limitations Section Audit
[Quality assessment: specific and constructive]. The authors honestly acknowledge the limitations of their 'file-tuning' approach and the current focus on Lean 4.

#### Negative Results and Failure Modes
[Partially reported]. The failure of state-only models on miniCTX is a clear negative result that substantiates the need for the benchmark.

#### Scope Verdict
The claims match the evidence for the importance of context, but the benchmark's difficulty for current long-context models is likely even higher than the baselines suggest.

#### Overall Completeness Verdict
Substantially complete and a significant service to the ITP community.

### Open Questions
- How does the accuracy of context-dependent proving scale with model parameter count versus context window size?
- Can NTP-TOOLKIT be extended to other interactive theorem provers like Coq or Isabelle with similar ease?
- Does the use of natural language comments in the context provide a measurable benefit for proof generation?
